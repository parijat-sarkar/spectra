What does this report?

Beagle reports one row per guide, assuming all editable bases in the window are edited simultaneously. Spectra reports one row per partial edit combination, so a guide with 3 A's in the window gives 7 outcomes (A, B, C, AB, AC, BC, ABC), each with its own HGVS nucleotide and amino-acid consequence.

Columns 1-24 match Beagle's output format. Three columns are added at the end: Edit Combination, Num Edits in Combination, Total Combinations for Guide.

What Spectra does NOT do: probability weighting. Partial outcomes are not ranked by predicted frequency. Integrate BE-Hive (maxwshen/be_predict_bystander) if you want that.
